About GERI

The Global Ecosystem Research Infrastructure (GERI) is an integrated, international network of site-based research infrastructures working to advance understanding of ecosystem function and change across global biomes. By providing harmonized ecological data from long-term observation sites, GERI supports high-impact scientific research and enables informed decision-making to address pressing environmental and societal challenges.

GERI fosters collaboration among its six founding continental-scale partners, facilitating new insights into global ecological processes that span regions, disciplines, and decades. Through open data practices, standardized methodologies, and a shared commitment to interoperability, GERI enables the scientific and education communities with comprehensive data to respond more effectively to climate change, biodiversity loss, and ecological hazards.

Founding GERI Partners

On December 8, 2020, six leading continental-scale ecosystem research infrastructures signed a landmark Memorandum of Understanding, officially establishing GERI.

Why GERI?

While each ERI produces valuable data at the national or regional level, addressing global environmental challenges like climate hazards, ecosystem degradation, and carbon dynamics requires globally coordinated, harmonized data. GERI bridges this gap by:

  • Harmonizing ecosystem data across networks and regions
  • Standardizing metadata, units, and semantic ontologies
  • Enabling cross-site synthesis and comparative ecological research
  • Applying FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) Principles

Data on this Portal

This portal provides access to a prototype of harmonized GERI data products, focusing on ecological drought, as a use case, including:

  • Precipitation
  • Air Temperature
  • Soil Moisture
  • Soil Temperature
  • Soil Texture

All datasets have undergone rigorous harmonization workflows across infrastructures, ensuring consistency in spatial, temporal, and methodological dimensions. The data are available for download, discovery, and integration via both the GERI DataONE Portal and the DataONE API.

Looking Ahead

GERI continues to evolve through collaborative governance, open science practices, and early-career engagement. As the global infrastructure expands, additional data products and analytical tools will further support interdisciplinary ecosystem research, decision-making, and climate resilience efforts.
